Perseus "Percy" Jackson is the protagonist of Rick Riordan’s Percy Jackson & the Olympians series. He is a demigod, the son of the mortal Sally Jackson and Poseidon, the Greek god of the sea. Percy discovers his true identity as a teenager after being attacked by monsters, leading him to Camp Half-Blood. He suffers from ADHD and dyslexia, which are actually signs of his demigod reflexes and brain wiring for Ancient Greek. As a son of one of the "Big Three" gods, he possesses immense power, including control over water, enhanced strength, and the ability to cause earthquakes. He is accompanied on quests by his best friend Grover Underwood, a satyr, and Annabeth Chase, a daughter of Athena. Percy wields "Riptide," a magical sword that transforms from a ballpoint pen. Throughout the series, he plays a crucial role in defeating the Titan lord Kronos and saving Mount Olympus. He later becomes a major character in The Heroes of Olympus series, where he displays significant character growth and maturity. Known for his intense loyalty, Percy is a skilled, brave, and humorous hero who often risks his life to protect his friends and family.
